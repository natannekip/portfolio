## ♿ My Accessibility Philosophy

[cite_start]Accessibility is not a "check-the-box" task—it is a fundamental aspect of high-quality design. My approach is rooted in the following principles:

1. **Pedagogical Clarity:** If a student cannot navigate a course, they cannot learn the material. [cite_start]Accessibility is the foundation of usability.
2. [cite_start]**Proactive Empathy:** Using screen readers (NVDA, Narrator) and manual testing isn't just about compliance; it's about understanding the diverse ways users experience information.
3. **Actionable Training:** I believe in empowering educators. [cite_start]By breaking down WCAG standards into "Pitstops" and visual guides, we make digital equity achievable for everyone.
